Galileo (Danti) Astrolabe

This large astrolabe, displayed at the Museo Galileo in Florence, was originally made by Egnazio Danti but is popularly known as the Galileo Astrolabe.

Designed for the latitude of Florence, it is regarded as a symbol of Renaissance scientific understanding and mathematical observational method.

PeriodRenaissance, 16th century
Place of ManufactureFlorence, Italy
Original MakerEgnazio Danti
MaterialMetal
Reference CollectionMuseo Galileo, Florence
