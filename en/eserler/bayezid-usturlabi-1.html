Sultan Bayezid II's Astrolabe

Sultan Bayezid II's Astrolabe is an important example of the Ottoman Empire's early scientific and astronomical work, used for calculating prayer times.

Among the astronomical instruments of its era, it stands out for its elegant craftsmanship and detailed mathematical measurements.

Date1505–1506
Place of ManufactureOttoman Empire (Istanbul)
ArtistAl-Ahmar al-Nujumi al-Rumi
MaterialBrass
DiameterApprox. 18 cm
