Korean Astrolabe (Bay Togiya)

This astrolabe holds a unique place as the only true rotating astrolabe known to have been made in East Asia and to survive to this day.

The body of the astrolabe was designed for 38° north latitude, corresponding to observatories near Seoul.

DateEstimated 1700s
PeriodJoseon Dynasty (1392–1897)
DesignerTeacher Yag-am, Yun
MaterialBrass
DiameterApprox. 16.9 cm
Reference CollectionJeonbuk National Museum, Jeonju, South Korea
